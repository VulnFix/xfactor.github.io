Why all this?

Instead of traditional 5G, LTE, or WiFi, Our Hotspot device uses LoRaWAN (Long Range Wide Area Network) enhancing the IoT Network, A low frequency wireless network powered through the blockchain.

Current communication technology

IoT devices need to wirelessly connect to the Internet and Geolocate. Conventional cellular networks are prone to frequent downtimes, would incur high usage costs, high power usage and Lower ranges of coverage. These are the limiting factors of IoT ecosystems expansion.

Meet LoRaWAN. A superior alternative with Low Power-High Range devices.

The cheaper, energy conscious, secure little brother to 5G, LTE, and WiFi. Running at 865Mhz, LoRaWAN has the ability to connect low-power devices that transfer lower amounts of data for far less the cost of traditional Telecommunication networks.

The hotspot is a small hardware device that creates a large wireless network or necesary coverage for devices that need Internet. Think of it like a Wi-Fi router, but for devices sending small amounts of data over long ranges. These devices are part of what's usually called the IoT — or the “Internet of Things.” The Hotspot creates a Long-Fi radio signal and routes packets from low-power devices in your area that use LoRaWAN and have been deployed to the Network. Typically these are devices such as GPS trackers, environmental sensors, weather meters, etc., that only need to transmit and share small bits of information. The Hotspot uses your existing Internet (via WiFi or Ethernet) to deliver the data packets sent by devices. It does not replace internet or cellular service for regular devices like computers and smartphones.
